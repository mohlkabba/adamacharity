Who We Are

A foundation rooted in compassion

Adama's Relief & Rehabilitation Foundation is a humanitarian organisation based in Sierra Leone, serving the most vulnerable members of our community — widows, elderly individuals, and families living with disabilities.

We believe that aid should be dignified, direct, and driven by community. Every programme we run is designed not just to provide short-term relief, but to build the conditions for long-term independence.

Founded with a simple but powerful idea — that no one in our community should go without food, support, or hope — we have grown from a small local initiative into an organised foundation with clear goals, growing campaigns, and a dedicated volunteer base.

Our Foundation

Mission, Vision & Values

Our Mission

To provide humanitarian relief and long-term rehabilitation support to vulnerable people — including widows, the elderly, and families with disabilities — through food assistance, assistive equipment, and sustained community support.

Our Vision

A community where every person — regardless of age, ability, or circumstance — has access to food, dignity, and the support they need to live independently and with purpose.

Our Values

Dignity in every interaction. Transparency with every donor. Community ownership in every programme. Long-term thinking behind every decision.

Community-First

Every decision is made by and for the communities we serve. Local knowledge, local care, local trust.

Long-term Thinking

Relief today, independence tomorrow — we design programmes that build futures, not just address moments.

Transparency

We are open about how donations are used. Every parcel, every pound, every person reached is accounted for.

Dignity in Aid

We treat every recipient as a person deserving of respect — not a number on a list or a photo opportunity.

Founder Story

Why Adama's Foundation was built

Adama's Relief & Rehabilitation Foundation was born from a simple, deeply personal conviction: that the most vulnerable people in our community — widows raising children alone, elderly neighbours with no family nearby, disabled individuals with no access to support — deserved more than they were getting.

The Foundation was built by people who had seen the gap between what existed and what was needed. Not just food for a day — but consistent, dignified, organised support that acknowledged people's humanity.

The Ramadan 2026 campaign — reaching over 50 families on 27th February — was our first large-scale distribution. It proved what we had always believed: that community-led, community-funded aid, delivered with care, works.

"We started because someone had to. We continue because everyone deserves to be seen."
— Adama's Relief & Rehabilitation Foundation
